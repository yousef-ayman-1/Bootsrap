Popular distination in Europe

London
London, the capital of the United Kingdom, is a sprawling metropolis with a rich history spanning nearly two millennia. It's a global hub, exerting significant influence on world art, entertainment, fashion, commerce, finance, education, healthcare, media, science, technology, tourism, transport, and communications.
Moscow
Moscow, the capital and largest city of Russia, is a sprawling metropolis on the Moskva River in Central Russia. With a population exceeding 13 million within its city limits and over 20 million in its metropolitan area, it is the most populous city entirely in Europe and one of the world's largest cities by land area.

Popular Travel Destinations

Big Ben

The iconic clock tower of the Palace of Westminster in London, officially called Elizabeth Tower. Its 13-ton bell has kept time since 1859 and is a symbol of British democracy.

See Profile

Tower Bridge

This Victorian bascule and suspension bridge over the River Thames is one of London's most recognizable landmarks. Its twin towers and glass walkways offer stunning city views.

See Profile

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum in Paris, housing over 38,000 objects including the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo. Its glass pyramid entrance has become an architectural icon.

See Profile

Eiffel Tower

Paris's iron lattice tower built for the 1889 World's Fair. At 324 meters tall, it was the world's tallest man-made structure for 41 years and receives nearly 7 million visitors annually.

See Profile

Moscow Kremlin

A fortified complex at the heart of Moscow, serving as the official residence of the Russian President. Its red walls contain cathedrals, palaces, and the famous Tsar Bell.

See Profile

St. Basil's Cathedral

This 16th-century Moscow cathedral with its colorful onion domes was built by Ivan the Terrible. Its unique architecture has no parallel in Russian tradition.

See Profile

Trevi Fountain

Rome's largest Baroque fountain, famous for its legend that throwing a coin over your shoulder ensures a return to Rome. It stands 26 meters high and 49 meters wide.

See Profile

Colosseum

The largest ancient amphitheater ever built, located in Rome. This elliptical wonder could hold 50,000-80,000 spectators for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.

See Profile
